About 313

Emily Lindenbaum

Art Therpist

Emily graduated from of the Masters in Education Art Therapy program at Wayne State. She has a B.B.A in Marketing from the Seidman College of Business at Grand Valley State University. After receiving her degree, Emily moved to Detroit to work in advertising and pursue her M.Ed. in Art Therapy at WSU. Emily has worked as a Clinical Trial Research Coordinator under the direct supervision of Joel L. Young, MD at Rochester Center for Behavioral Medicine for over 5 years. She has experience conducting Adult and Pediatric research studies on CNS trials including: Major Depression Disorder, Treatment-Resistant Depression, Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder, Binge Eating Disorder, Generalized Anxiety Disorder, Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder, and Personality Disorders. Emily is a qualified clinician to administer assessments and scales of DSM-5 diagnoses when working with these individuals 1-v-1 during patient visits and upholding the safety and efficacy of her patients. In her clinical experience, she has a range of experience working with different populations throughout her graduate program. Emily has experiencing facilitating Art Therapy groups and doing art and talk therapy with individuals both in-person and virtually. She worked as a student art therapist at the Henry Ford Cancer Institute Infusion Center doing individual and group art therapies with patients receiving chemotherapy/infusion treatments. She also co-facilitates a Veteranâ€™s Art Therapy Group at the Grosse Pointe War Memorial and is working on providing more opportunities there for children and young adults in the area. Emily designed several art therapy programs for social and emotional learning for Grosse Pointe South Schools with different high school aged students. Emily is interested in working with children, adolescents, adults and senior populations that would like to try a creative approach to traditional talk therapies. She believes in taking a Humanistic approach by also providing psychoeducational knowledge through using different art media. Emily enjoys photography, cooking, being on her boat in the summer, and hanging out with her dog, Henry in her free time.
